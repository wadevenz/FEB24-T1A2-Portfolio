Career


I started my professional career in healthcare, first working as a registered nurse before tranitioning into paramedicine. Being a paramedic was a dream come true and I loved my job.
But after almost seven years (and two kids) later, the shift work had started to take a toll. I was missing out on spending time with my kids, and maintaining a healthy routine was proving all but impossible. So, I needed to make a change.
My wife has worked in the software space for most of her career. She's closely with developers as a UX writer, and knows the industry inside-out. As crazy as the jump from paramedics to developer seemed, she convinced me that it would be a perfect fit for my skills and personality.
After studying to be a full stack developer with Coder Academy, I couldn't agree more. I get to flex my problem solving skills in a creative and technical environment.
